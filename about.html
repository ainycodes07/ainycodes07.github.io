How I got here
Started building.
Kept going.

I didn't start with a plan to do all of this. I started with one thing — learning how to build websites — and the rest followed naturally. Design made the sites look right. Writing made them say the right thing. Teaching made me actually understand what I was doing.

All three skills developed together, which is why they work well together when a client needs more than one. There's no handoff between "the designer" and "the developer" — it's the same person thinking about the whole thing at once.

"The work is cleaner when one person understands all the parts."

I'm early in my freelance career. Two clients, six projects, several self-initiated builds. I'm not pretending otherwise. What I can say is that the work is done properly, communicated clearly, and delivered on time.

How I work
Direct.
No performance.

I don't do calls unless necessary. I prefer written communication — it's clearer, documented, and doesn't waste either person's time. Most projects can be scoped, agreed on, and delivered entirely over email.

I work quietly and send updates when there's something to show. I ask one question when I need clarification, not five. I flag problems early instead of hoping they resolve themselves.

Communication
Email-first. Replies within 24–48 hours on weekdays. No disappearing mid-project.
Scope
Agreed upfront, in writing. Nothing changes without discussion.
Revisions
Two rounds included in every project. Additional rounds are scoped separately.
Delivery
On the agreed date. If something changes, I'll tell you before the deadline, not after.
Quality
I don't send rough drafts as finals. What you receive is complete and ready to use.
What I'm building toward
More work.
Better work.

Right now the focus is on taking on more projects, building a stronger portfolio, and developing the course side of things. More certifications are in progress. More client work is the goal.

I'm not trying to scale to an agency. I'm building a practice where the work is good, the clients are straightforward, and the output is something I'd put my name on — which I do, every time.
